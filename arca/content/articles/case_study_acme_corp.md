# How ACME Corp Streamlined Their Documentation with Arca

## Challenge

ACME Corporation, a global manufacturing leader with over 5,000 employees, faced significant challenges managing their technical documentation and internal knowledge base. Their existing content management solution had become:

- **Unwieldy**: The system required specialized training and dedicated administrators
- **Slow**: Content updates took days to publish due to approval bottlenecks
- **Inflexible**: Custom content types required developer intervention
- **Costly**: Annual licensing fees were increasing while user satisfaction decreased

Their technical writing team of 25 professionals was spending more time fighting with the CMS than creating valuable documentation for their products.

> "Our previous CMS was becoming a liability rather than an asset. Documentation updates couldn't keep pace with our product development cycle." - Maria Chen, Documentation Manager at ACME

## Solution

After evaluating several alternatives, ACME chose Arca for its simplicity, flexibility, and file-based approach to content management. The implementation involved:

1. **Migration**: Existing content was converted to Markdown with YAML frontmatter
2. **Git Integration**: Documentation was stored in Git, aligning with their development workflow
3. **CI/CD Pipeline**: Automated publishing through their existing CI/CD tools
4. **Custom Templates**: Development of templates specific to their various document types
5. **Training**: A 2-hour workshop for the technical writing team

## Results

Six months after implementing Arca, ACME reported:

- **90% reduction** in time-to-publish for documentation updates
- **73% increase** in documentation contributions from engineering teams
- **35% decrease** in support tickets related to outdated documentation
- **$175,000 annual savings** in licensing and administrative costs

The technical writing team now works directly in their preferred text editors, and version control has dramatically improved content quality through peer reviews and change tracking.

> "Arca transformed our documentation process. Our teams now think of documentation as code, with all the benefits that brings - version control, easy collaboration, and automated publishing." - James Wilson, CTO at ACME

## Key Benefits

- **Developer-friendly workflow**: Engineers now contribute directly to documentation
- **Content versioning**: Complete history of all documentation changes
- **Simplified review process**: Git pull requests for documentation reviews
- **Reduced infrastructure**: No complex database or specialized servers required
- **Improved security**: Content storage aligned with secure code practices

## Conclusion

By adopting Arca's file-based approach to content management, ACME Corporation transformed their documentation process from a bottleneck into a competitive advantage. The simplicity and flexibility of Markdown files with Git version control allowed their team to focus on creating valuable content rather than managing a complex CMS.

ACME continues to expand their use of Arca, now implementing it for marketing content and internal knowledge management.
